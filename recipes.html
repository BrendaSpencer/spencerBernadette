I try to balance fresh food with frozen food because it just makes it all a little bit easier. like chopped unions and garlic, it saves me time, smelly fingers and no more forgotten unions in my pantry. Hope you like them as much as my family does

Tasty Water

  • 1 citron
  • 1lemon
  • 1 orange
  • 4 twigs of mint
  • ice cubes
  • honey or grenadine
  • lots of water

Wash the mint and citrus, cut the citrus in slices. put it all in a nice jar or bottle with some ice and lots of water. And if you like it a little sweeter just put in some honey or grenadine. Enjoy♥

Pastasauce

  • 600gr minced meat
  • can of chopped tomatoes or fresh.
  • 600gr of chopped paprikas fresh or frozen.
  • unions and garlic to your liking
  • laurel and thyme
  • olive oil and pasta

Bake the meat together with the union nice and brown. Put the paprikas with the meat and bake for a few minutes then put the tomatoes in and let it simmer with the lit on. meanwhile put some water on for the pasta and boil it like its said on the package. spice up your sauce to your liking with the garlic, laurel and thyme. serve with some grated cheese.

mignonette, potatoe and carrots

  • 1 kg potatoes
  • 1 kg carrots
  • 2 shallots (fresh or frozen)
  • 4 porc mignonette
  • oliveoil
  • butter
  • salt, pepper, nutmeg, parsley

Peel the potatoes and the carrots. wash and cook the potatoes in plenty of water until they are done then drain them. chop the carrots in pieces. Put 2 ts oliveoil in a pot and glace the chopped shallots. stir the carrots under them. pour some water in it with a pinch of salt and put the lit on. stir regularly until the carrots are easy to prick in. melt some butter in a grillpan, spice up the meat with salt, pepper and nutmeg. bake them about 2 to 3 minutes each side depending on the thickness. finish it off with some parsley.

porc cheeks in a goulash twist

  • 800 gr porc cheeks
  • 2 shallots (or a hand full of chopped frozen ones)
  • 3 cloves of garlic (or again some from the freezer)
  • 5 tomatoes (or a can of chopped tomatoes 400gr)
  • 1 lime
  • 2 red, 2 green, 2 yellow paprika
  • herbs: cumin, parsley, salt, pepper
  • maizena express dark sauce
  • ebly
  • 1 bouillon cube

Sear the cheeks in a pot and let them bake until they are nice and brown. Then put the shallots in and bake for a few minutes before the fresh tomatoes go in, if you use a can of tomatoes then also put 300ml of water in, let simmer for 1h. remove the seeds from the paprika and chop in big pieces, stir them into the sauce. again let it simmer for 15 min, then put in to your own flavour the garlic, some cumin, salt and pepper and a quarter of the lime. Put on water to boil with a cube of bouillon. when it boils you can put the ebly in. follow the instructions on the box. Before serving put over some oliveoil. when the cheeks are nice and tender, you can stir in the maizena to thicken the sauce, again this should be done to your own liking. Before serving put on some parsley freshly chopped, frozen or dried.

chicken and potatoes gratin

  • 500gr chicken filet chopped and baked
  • 300gr frozen brocoli
  • 300gr frozen cauliflower
  • 400gr sliced potatoes
  • 150gr gratin cheese
  • butter
  • cream
  • flour
  • garlic powder, parsley, bouilion cube

Arange the chicken and the vegtebales in a dish and put the sliced potatoes on top. Melt the butter in a pot and mix it with the flour until its creamy. The more butter is used the more flour is needed and more sauce you’ll have. then pour in the cream with the cube and keep firmly stirring. Add water until you have the wanted texture. Put in the herbs to your liking and let it gently boil for a few minutes. Keep watch on the thickness of the sauce ans stir regullarly. Pour over the potatoes sprinkle the cheese on top and put in the oven for 40 min 200°C.
